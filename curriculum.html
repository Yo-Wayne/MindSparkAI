Full Learning Curriculum

We teach AI literacy through hands-on, structured modules. Each project is a step toward becoming thoughtful, creative, and future-ready learners.

Module Title Objectives Student Project Example
1 What is AI, Really? Help students build an informed understanding of how AI works and how to ask clear, purposeful questions. Students will gain the confidence to use AI more thoughtfully in everyday tasks. Ask AI fun questions like “Why do cats purr?” and learn how to get better answers by asking clearer questions.
2 Build With AI Guide students through building websites, simple apps, and interactive projects using AI-assisted tools. Introduce block-based coding to develop problem-solving and digital creation skills. Create a personal “About Me” website or a simple interactive game with step-by-step AI prompts.
3 Smart Studying Teach students how to use AI to summarize, outline, and explore academic content more deeply. They’ll learn to study smarter, ask better questions, and grow as independent learners. Turn a long article into a concise study guide, generate flashcards, and craft focused summaries.
4 Responsible Creators Support students in creating original projects—art, music, writing, or personal websites—while learning to use AI ethically and intentionally. Emphasis is placed on self-expression and creativity with purpose. Design a digital poster on a social topic or compose a short music piece with AI guidance.
5 Present + Persuade Train students to plan, polish, and deliver impactful presentations using AI for writing, slide design, and video/audio practice. Develop real-world communication skills needed to pitch ideas, promote products, and present themselves with clarity and confidence. Build a 3-slide pitch on “Why AI Matters” with AI-enhanced visuals and practiced narration.
6 Capstone (With Tracks) Empower students to choose a focus area—web, art, media, or study tools—and create a final project that reflects their interests and skills. This encourages self-discovery, initiative, and deeper skill development. Develop a personal website, interactive art installation, or AI study assistant as a showcase project.

Learning Progression

Each module builds on the previous one, creating a comprehensive AI literacy foundation

Conceptual Understanding

Help students understand what AI is (and isn’t) by guiding them through real examples.

Research & Communication

Show students how to use AI to gather, refine, and express ideas clearly.

Active Problem-Solving

Train students to use AI as a thinking partner for solving problems step-by-step.

Creative Expression

Introduce AI tools that help students transform ideas into visual and artistic works.

Technical Foundations

Give students hands-on experience with AI-assisted coding and basic model training.

Multimedia Fluency

Equip students with skills to create polished slides, videos, and audio using AI-enhanced tools.

Integration & Innovation

Support students in choosing a focus area and building a complete capstone project.
